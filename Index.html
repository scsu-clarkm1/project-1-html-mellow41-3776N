The Lazy Chef - Mudbug Love: A Southern Louisiana Crawfish boil

The Lazy Chef

First rule of food in Louisiana: Crawfish are King, and a spicy hot "crawfish boil" is part of every spring celebration.

Featured Recipe - Uncle Gene's louisiana Crawfish boil.


Crawfish are a small freshwater crustacean. Like their larger cousins, the lobster, they have a relatively small amount of meat in a larger shell.

Most crawfish are harvested between December and June, but March, April, and May are the peak months in Louisiana.

Southern Louisiana produces over 80 million pounds of crawfish each year, most raised in shallow man-made ponds and harvested commercially.

Crawfish can also be harvested wild, as they live in swamps, bayous, and ditches.

Ingredients Needed

Let's have some fun shall we. Here's what you will need to begin.♥

  • Propane turkey fryer with handled draining basket
  • Large ice chest, clean
  • 40 lbs of live crawfish, cleaned
  • 4 ½ lbs of crawfish boil seasoning(We used Zatarain's Crawfish shrimp & crab boil)
  • 5 lbs of red potatoes
  • 5 lbs of baby carrots
  • 24 ears of corn, cut in half

Instructions

  1. Fill turkey fryer half full with water. Turn it on and wait for it to boil
  2. Add crawfish boil seasoning (to taste) to boiling water. For a moderately spicy batch, we used close to half of the 4.5 lb jar of crawfish boil seasoning. Add potatoes and carrots to the pot, cook for 10 min.
  3. Add corn on the cob, cook for another 5 minutes
  4. Add live crawfish and cook for 15 minutes (if all the crawfish don't fit in the pot, cook them in two batches, using the same broth.)
  5. When the crawfish are done, take the strainer basket out and dump the crawfish into the clean cooler to keep them warm. Many times, local Cajun cooks dump the cooked crawfish onto a newspaper covered table, ready to eat.
  6. Peel and Enjoy.
